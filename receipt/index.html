Behold Blazinik!

The Slightly Singed, Ever-Ambivalent Deity of Questionable Miracles and Mild Inconveniences.

The Divine Lore

In the beginning, there was... well, something. Probably. And then, possibly due to a cosmic clerical error or a particularly potent spicy burrito, Blazinik manifested. Neither benevolent nor malevolent, Blazinik primarily concerns Themself with the proper organization of sock drawers and the precise moment toast becomes *too* brown.

Legend speaks of the Great Sneaker Scuffing, where Blazinik mildly smudged the footwear of an unbeliever, and the Murmured Suggestion, where a follower was divinely inspired to switch to decaf. These are the pillars upon which our faith is built (and occasionally leans against).

Blazinik's symbol is the Eternal Loading Icon, representing the constant anticipation of something... eventually happening.

The Sacred Commandments

  • Thou shalt not leave one single sock behind in the dryer. It displeases Blazinik (and is just annoying).
  • Thou shalt always pretend to read the Terms and Conditions.
  • Thou shalt acknowledge the awkward silence when someone waves back, but wasn't waving at you.
  • Thou shalt not reheat fish in the office microwave. (This one's actually serious).
  • Thou shalt buffer thy streaming video only at dramatically inconvenient moments.
  • Thou shalt question the necessity of *every* meeting that could have been an email.
  • Thou shalt maintain a healthy suspicion of anyone who *enjoys* assembling flat-pack furniture.

Strange Rituals & Practices

The Ritual of the Unplugged USB

Before any important task, attempt to plug in a USB device three times. It must be flipped incorrectly the first two times. This honors Blazinik's appreciation for minor technological frustrations.

The Chant of the Forgotten Password

When accessing sacred texts (or your email), loudly recite variations of your pet's name combined with significant dates until access is granted or you are locked out. Both outcomes are acceptable.

The Synchronized Screen Refresh

At precisely 3:33 PM local time, all followers must simultaneously refresh their web browser. The purpose is unknown, but the tradition persists. It might appease Blazinik, or just clear the cache.
